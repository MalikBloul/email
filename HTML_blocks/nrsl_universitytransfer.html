Transferring to Macquarie University

To transfer to Macquarie University, you will need to apply via UAC and provide your current tertiary marks, your final secondary school marks, and any other studies you have completed. These will be used to determine whether you meet the requirements for entry into your preferred degree at Macquarie. UAC will generate a selection rank based on these results, which Macquarie will use to assess your application. Please keep in mind that the higher your previous tertiary marks are, the more competitive your application is likely to be. More information about transferring to Macquarie can be found here.

Once you have received and accepted an offer, you can apply for Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) to be assessed for credit for your previous studies.  RPL is granted on a case by case basis by the university, so we cannot provide any specific information about your eligibility for RPL in the new degree at Macquarie. You may be eligible for specified credit for completed units equivalent to units offered at Macquarie University.  Our credit transfer register provides a list of units that have previously been accepted for credit by the University.  This is not an exhaustive list and units may still be accepted if they are not on the list.  It may also be useful to compare the unit guides for each unit required of the new course with those of the units you are trying to get credit for, to assess how similar they are. You may also be eligible for unspecified credit toward the elective component and general requirements of a degree (where no specific equivalent Macquarie unit exists).

We recommend that you apply for any RPL you think may be applicable, once you have accepted an offer to study at Macquarie University. More information about RPL at Macquarie University can be found here.

If you are concerned that you may not meet the entry requirements for your preferred degree, Macquarie offers a number of alternative entry pathways that you can find here.
